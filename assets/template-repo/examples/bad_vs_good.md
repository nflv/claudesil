# Bad vs Good Rules

## Bad

"Write clean code."

Problem: vague, not enforceable, teaches nothing.

## Good

"Never modify unrelated files during a focused bug fix."

Why it works: concrete, testable, and easy to review.

## Bad

"Test everything."

Problem: unrealistic and non-specific.

## Good

"Run the narrowest meaningful check first, then expand verification only if the local fix passes."

Why it works: clear sequence, lower cost, stronger discipline.
